Penalties are assessed for two, five, or ten minutes depending on the type of infraction. Penalties fall into the following categories:

Penalties

  • Minor
  • Major
  • Misconduct
  • Game Misconduct
  • Match

Officials penalize an offending player and his team by putting him in the penalty box. When a team has a player in the penalty box, they may not substitute another player in his place.

No matter how many penalties a team gets they can only be down two players at a time. Goalies can be penalized but they do not serve their penalties in the penalty box, a teammate chosen by his team sits in the penalty box instead.

When an offending team has to play at a disadvantage they are shorthanded on a penalty kill, the team that did not take a penalty is on a power play.


The Fouls

Physical Fouls

  • Boarding

    Boarding

    Checking or pushing a defenseless opponent that causes them to hit the boards violently.

  • Charging

    Charging

    A player who, as a result of distance traveled, violently checks an opponent.

  • Checking from Behind
  • Clipping

    Clipping

    Throwing the body, from any direction, across or below the knees of an opponent.
